Agronomy Workshop: A Golf Shirt with a Hidden Tee Slot and Nothing to Prove

Rob Junge designed exactly one shirt, put a hidden tee holder in the chest pocket, and called it a golf brand. The mock neck heavyweight work shirt — 17.5 oz cotton, made in LA — is the kind of garment that makes you wonder why anyone ever thought polyester was the answer to anything. No logos on the front. No moisture-wicking claims. Just fabric that weighs more than your commitment to keeping score.

The hats come with a pencil holder and an actual pencil, because Agronomy Workshop is the rare brand that remembers you need to write things down on a golf course. The towel is called "Unusual Lies" and it won't improve yours. Twelve SKUs total, three categories, zero hype — a catalog so small it fits on a scorecard, which is probably the point.
