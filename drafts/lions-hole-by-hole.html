Handicap 6 — "Hogan's Hole"
The most famous hole at Muny. Ben Hogan reportedly stood on this tee and asked, "Where's the fairway?" The drive is blind — you're hitting over a ridge with no idea what's on the other side until you've committed. The approach crosses water to an elevated, sloping green. Even at 381 from the whites, it plays much harder than the handicap suggests. This is the hole you'll talk about afterward.
Local tip Ask the group ahead where they aimed off the tee, or find a regular and follow their lead. There's a safe landing area slightly left of center that most locals play to. On the approach, commit to your club — the water punishes indecision more than it punishes bad swings.

A hundred years of keeping it public

Lions Municipal isn't just the oldest muni in Texas — it's one of the most important golf courses in the American South. Before Brown v. Board of Education, before the Civil Rights Act, two young Black men walked onto this course and started playing. Mayor Taylor Glass's response — "Let them play" — made Muny one of the first desegregated public golf courses in the South.

Harvey Penick learned the game here, then spent 50 years teaching it. Ben Crenshaw grew up playing these fairways. Ben Hogan left his name on the 16th hole. The Firecracker Open — Texas's oldest amateur tournament — has been played here since the 1940s.

When development threatened the course in the 2010s, the Austin community fought to save it. The Muny Conservancy was established, the course was listed on the National Register of Historic Places, and the land was preserved. Muny isn't a museum — it's a living course, $30 green fees, open to anyone who shows up.

  • 1924Austin Lions Club opens a 9-hole course on UT's Brackenridge Tract — the first public course in Austin
  • 1927Harvey Penick becomes head professional. He stays for 34 years.
  • 1936City of Austin assumes the lease from the Lions Club
  • ~1950Muny desegregates — one of the first public courses in the South. "Let them play."
  • 1940s+The Firecracker Open begins — Texas's oldest amateur tournament, still played annually
  • 2016Listed on the National Register of Historic Places and named to the National Trust's 11 Most Endangered list
  • 2019The Muny Conservancy is established to preserve the course for future generations
