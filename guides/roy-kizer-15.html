Roy Kizer Hole 15 — the prettiest
par 3 you'll ever bogey.

Short, scenic, and sneaky. At 137 from the tips it plays like a wedge — but the wind and the bunkers guarding the green make club selection a mind game. The easiest hole on the card by handicap, yet somehow half the foursome walks away with bogey.

Three ways to play it
Safe
Aim fat, stay safe.
9-iron to the center of the green. Ignore the pin, ignore the bunkers, just find the putting surface. At 137 this is a stock short iron — take the extra club and swing smooth. The green is wider than it looks from the tee. Two-putt from 30 feet and walk to 16 with a par and a clean card.
Tee: 9-Iron · Center green
Fun
Ride the wind, trust the number.
It's only 137, so this is wedge territory for most players. Take a smooth PW and shape a baby draw at the left third of the green. The wind usually crosses left-to-right, so start it at the left edge and let it drift. When it works, the ball tracks toward the pin like it's on a rail. Short par 3s reward tempo over muscle.
Tee: PW · Left edge, draw
Aggressive
Flag-hunt past the bunker.
When the pin is tucked behind the bunker, that's your moment. Gap wedge, high fade, land it three paces past the pin and let the slope feed it back. Miss right and you're in the sand. Miss long and you're chipping downhill off the back. But stick it and you're looking at a four-footer for birdie on the best-looking hole on the south side.
Tee: GW · High fade, pin-high
